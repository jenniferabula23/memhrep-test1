Festivals are occasions for re-union, remembrance and merry-making. In Ghana and Africa in general, festivals are also times where the dead are remembered through organised rituals such as libation, animal sacrifice and mourning. Occupying a significant place on the traditional calendar of the people of the Asante Kingdom of Ghana, is the Akwasidae festival. The festival commemorates the time when the Ashanti Golden Stool was magically brought down from heaven and it is observed on Sundays once every six weeks.

On Sunday 11th June 2023, staff of MEMHREP- GHANA, an NGO established about 11 years ago and located in Kumasi and dedicated to ending the plight as well as the distress of persons living with mental illness and other vulnerabilities joined the chiefs and people of Asante Juaben to celebrate the Akwasidae festival. The event which was highly patronised, saw in attendance the paramount chief of Asante Juaben, sub chiefs, opinion leaders and people of the community. Speaking at the event, the paramount chief Nana Otuo Siriboe who was clad in a traditional regalia, asked for the unity of the Asante tribe for national unity and the development of the area. He also called for the preservation of the customs and traditions of the people of Asante Juaben since it’s a tool for tourism in the country.

He took the event to appreciate the wonderful work of MEMHREP has been doing in addressing the needs of persons living with mental illness and called for social support. During the celebration, staff of MEMHREP- GHANA accompanied by the director of the Department of Social Welfare (Kumasi Metropolitan Assembly- KMA) presented items such as bottles of Schnapps, foreign drinks, soft drinks and bottled water to the palace of the paramount chief.

The Chief Executive Officer ( CEO ) of MEMHREP, Mr Adu-Gyamfi praised the Juabenhene for all the support offered the NGO during the past eleven( 11) years. He was particular about the need for education to curb the causes of mental illness amongst the general public especially the youth. He therefore asked individuals, religious bodies and corporate Ghana to support MEMHREP in its desire to establish a 500- bed modern rehabilitation centre at Juaben Asante to care for the mentally ill in Ghana.
