Mental health education ( MHE):

We tackle the root causes of mental illness by educating the general public through seminars, schools, religious and social gatherings, workplaces and workshops.






Community mental health support (CMHS):

With our team of medical professionals, dedicated and committed volunteers, persons with mental health needs are evaluated, assessed and given the needed medical help.






Mental health outreach :

This program is aimed at assisting persons who have mental illness to carefully and intensively recover. Also these persons are fed, groomed and clothed.






Complimentary home support and volunteering:

Our team of volunteers pay regular home visits to people with mental illness and epilepsy to find out firsthand what challenges they are facing at home or in the community.






Agricultural program:

Acres of land already acquired is used to produce food crops to feed persons suffering from mental illness. Also proceeds from the sale of our farm produce are used to acquire medical consumables.






FEE program:

This is a support strategy designed to reduce mental illness in a particular area where persons suffering from symptoms of mental illness can be located. This approach is also designed to reduce mental illness in areas where drugs and alcohol are abused.





COMMUNITY SUPPORT:

We also provide community support to those with acute/ enduring physical or mental health needs that includes home/hospital visitation, etc. and working in collaboration with other partner agencies to promote health and wellbeing.
