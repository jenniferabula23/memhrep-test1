People who suffer from Mental illness are widespread across the world. About 25 percent of the world's population have this diagnosis, but there are immense debates about mental illness, the causes, and the required steps leading to the total healing. People who are suffering from this kind of illness sometimes encounter difficulties in either their thinking abilities, their emotions or activities. These at times could lead to the destruction of the efficiency, relationships and quality of life of these people. The outcomes of this illness have mostly been unbearable for the family and friends, as well as the patients.


People who are looked down upon in their everyday life, as a result of suffering from mental illness, are uncountable. This is because, mental illnesses are some of the least meaningful situation in the society. However, unlike the images often found in articles, books, on television and in films, most people can lead generative and efficient lives when given good treatment and assistance. Drugs and medical treatments are good for some people, but this is not always the cure. Medical treatment may play a part in the recovery process, and not necessarily the main part.
It should be noted that someone with mental illness is not the person's mistake, it's not a sign of weakness, and it's not something to be embarrassed of. It's too narrow to position someone's problems solely as an illness that requires medical treatment. As a result of this, people are prevented from thinking about the many different influences on someone's life, thoughts, feelings and behaviour that can cause mental distress. It may also prevent people from exploring the different kinds of non-medical treatment choices that are available. For these reasons, some people prefer to talk about mental or emotional distress, rather than mental illness.






Cause of Mental Health

Mental health is influenced by a complex interplay of factors, including biological, psychological, and environmental factors. Here are some of the key causes of mental health issues:


1. Genetics: Mental health conditions can run in families, suggesting a genetic component to some conditions. For example, individuals with a family history of depression may be more likely to experience depression themselves.


2. Life experiences: Traumatic life experiences such as abuse, neglect, or loss can increase the risk of mental health conditions such as PTSD, depression, or anxiety. Other life experiences such as chronic stress or discrimination may also contribute to the development of mental health issues.


3. Brain chemistry: Imbalances in neurotransmitters or other brain chemicals can contribute to the development of mental health conditions. For example, low levels of serotonin have been linked to depression.


4.Substance abuse: Substance abuse can increase the risk of mental health conditions and exacerbate existing conditions. Environmental factors: Environmental factors such as poverty, social isolation, and lack of access to resources can contribute to the development of mental health issues.

It's important to remember that mental health issues are complex and multifaceted. Many factors can contribute to the development of mental health conditions, and treatment often involves a multifaceted approach that addresses the various underlying factors contributing to the issue. Seeking support from mental health professionals and engaging in healthy coping strategies can help individuals manage and overcome mental health challenges.



Measure To Be taken to Improve mental health

Improving mental health involves taking proactive steps to prioritize one's well-being and address any mental health concerns. Here are some measures that individuals can take to improve their mental health:


1.Seek professional support: Mental health professionals such as therapists or psychiatrists can help individuals manage mental health conditions and develop healthy coping strategies.


2.Practice self-care: Engaging in activities such as exercise, getting enough sleep, eating a healthy diet, and practicing relaxation techniques such as meditation or yoga can help promote mental well-being.


3.Connect with others: Maintaining social connections and building supportive relationships with friends and family can help reduce feelings of isolation and improve mental health.


4.Manage stress: Engaging in stress-reducing activities such as exercise, spending time in nature, or practicing mindfulness can help manage stress and prevent the development of mental health issues.


5. Avoid substance abuse: Substance abuse can exacerbate mental health conditions and increase the risk of developing mental health issues. Avoiding alcohol and drugs can help improve mental well-being.


6.Address any underlying issues: Addressing underlying issues such as trauma, unresolved grief, or relationship difficulties can help prevent the development of mental health issues or help manage existing mental health conditions.


7. Prioritize work-life balance: Ensuring a healthy balance between work, leisure, and other aspects of life can help reduce stress and promote mental well-being.


Overall, taking proactive steps to prioritize mental health and seek support when needed can help individuals manage mental health concerns and improve their overall well-being.
