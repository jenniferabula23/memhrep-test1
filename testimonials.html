Our Testimonials




Agnes Takyiwaa

Trader

I was mentally ill, a drug addict and as a result abandoned on the streets of Adum - Kumasi ( Ghana) at the tender age of nine( 9). I was sexually abused and got pregnant with nobody to cater for my medical and physical needs. I came in contact with MEMHREP during one of their street outreach programs at Asafo - Kumasi. They paid my ante and post natal bills, provided materially and to my outmost surprise, housed me till I delivered and beyond. As if that was not enough, six months after delivery, they put me on training for work. Words alone cannot express the help rendered me by MEMHREP. All I can say is God richly bless you MEMHREP.

Yaw Antwi

Mechanic

In January 2017, I lost both parents mysteriously. It got my younger brother into a state of mental instability. I spent all my savings battling his illness but to no avail leaving me financially drained as well. Suddenly when all hope was lost, MEMHREP came to our aid. My brother was quickly enrolled into their rehabilitation program and in no time he started responding to treatment. Their carefully designed care plan coupled with counselling and prayer sessions turned my brother's pains into gains. Because of the timely intervention of MEMHREP, not only is he sane but employed as well. Our appreciation goes to MEMHREP for their wonderful work to humankind.

Sheila Aku

Secretary

I am a university graduate. I was in a sexual relationship with my uncle after the passing of my father. My situation required that I live with my uncle because the school I attended was very close to his house and more over he was sponsoring my education. Our sexual escapades lasted for years which got me depressed morally and spiritually. It also affected my school grades negatively . Fortunately, I encountered MEMHREP's online counselling session and I must say it was my "saving grace". Their approach was simple, professional and welcoming. I therefore want to use this opportunity to appeal for support on behalf of MEMHREP.

The victim having worked for six years as an apprentice managed to raise money in the quest to cater for the education of his fiancee. Jennifer Darko

Trader

I am a trader at the Kumasi Central Market. I was an eyewitness when on Easter Monday 2017, MEMHREP fed, clothed and gave medical help to over 100 mentally ill persons on the streets of Kumasi ( Railways). They also educated onlookers about the causes and prevention of mental illness and entreated the general public to stop the stigmatization associated with mental illness. Through this medium, I sincerely want to urge all Ghanaians and even beyond to come to the aid of this noble organization to redefine the lives of persons with mental health needs.
